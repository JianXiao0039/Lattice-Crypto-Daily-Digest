# Phase 13E ARS Paper Radar Release Gate Review

## Skill Use

The academic-paper-reviewer workflow was used only to audit track-boundary claims and overstatement. It did not assign human-gold labels or modify production code.

## Boundary Review

- Track A: generic signatures, commitments, and ZK are not sufficient without Module-SIS, lattice chameleon-hash, sanitizable-signature, exposure, or trapdoor evidence.
- Track B: author identity is prohibited as a positive feature; technical bridge relevance is required.
- Track C: generic AI, machine learning, computer vision, or optimization is excluded without lattice cryptanalysis evidence.
- Track D: broad PQC or scheme mentions must not make it a miscellaneous bucket.

## Claim Review

The reports do not claim that v0.2 improves accuracy. They state that it is a candidate design responding to observed non-gold diagnostic errors. The paper-radar core may be described as unchanged by this phase, but release readiness remains subject to local validation, current CI evidence, and durable-run requirements.
