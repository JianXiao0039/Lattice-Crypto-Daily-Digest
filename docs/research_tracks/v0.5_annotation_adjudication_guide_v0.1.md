# v0.5 Annotation Adjudication Guide v0.1

## Primary Track

Choose the track that best explains why the record is useful to the research radar. Use secondary tracks only when the retained evidence independently supports them.

## Ambiguity

Use `ambiguous` when multiple tracks remain plausible or technical centrality is unclear. Do not force balance across tracks.

## Irrelevance

Choose `irrelevant` for keyword collisions, non-lattice cryptography, generic ML, or generic privacy/signature/commitment work without a lattice/PQC anchor.

## Conflict Resolution

When production, shadow, and Codex review disagree:

1. prefer the retained abstract over title keywords;
2. prefer explicit constructions/assumptions over broad taxonomy tags;
3. record the decisive evidence;
4. mark `conflict_requires_adjudication` if evidence remains insufficient.

Reviewer identity and decision date should be recorded outside machine-proposed fields.
